# Employee Data Analysis Dashboard

## 📊 Project Overview
This interactive **Employee Data Analysis Dashboard** provides deep insights into the workforce of 1,470 employees. It highlights key HR metrics including demographics, tenure, promotion eligibility, retrenchment risk, job satisfaction, and performance levels.

**Total Employees**: 1,470  
**Male**: 882 | **Female**: 588  
**Average Employee Age**: 36.92 years

## 🔍 Key Analysis

### 1. Retrenchment Risk
- **117 employees** (8.0%) are flagged to be retrenched.
- **1,353 employees** (92.0%) will not be retrenched.
- A dedicated table lists all 117 employees at risk of retrenchment.

### 2. Promotion Eligibility
- **72 employees** (4.9%) are **Due for Promotion**.
- **1,398 employees** (95.1%) are **Not Due for Promotion**.

### 3. Service Years (Tenure Distribution)
- Highest concentration of employees have **5 years** of service (196 employees).
- Significant numbers in 1–3 years tenure bands, indicating a relatively young workforce in terms of company tenure.

### 4. Job Level & Involvement
- Majority of employees are in **Level 1 and Level 2** positions.
- Most employees handle **3 Jobs** simultaneously (high workload).

### 5. Satisfaction & Performance
- **Work Relationship Satisfaction**: High number of employees rated "Low".
- **Environment Satisfaction**: Moderate distribution across Low, Medium, and Very High.
- **Job Satisfaction**: Similar spread with a slight lean toward "Low".
- **Performance Rating**: 1,244 employees rated with **3 Stars**, only 226 with **4 Stars**.

### 6. Commute Distance
- **69.8%** of employees live **very far** from work.
- Only **13.88%** live **very close**.

---

## 💡 Key Insights

- The organization has a **young-to-mid age workforce** (avg. 36.92) but faces significant **promotion stagnation** (only 4.9% due for promotion).
- **8% retrenchment risk** signals potential cost-cutting or restructuring.
- High percentage of employees living **very far** from the workplace may be contributing to low satisfaction and higher turnover risk.
- Many employees are handling multiple job roles (3 Jobs), which could lead to burnout.
- Satisfaction levels (Job, Environment, and Work Relationship) lean toward the lower side, especially in work relationships.

---

## ⚠️ Problems Identified

1. **High Retrenchment Risk** – 117 employees targeted for exit.
2. **Promotion Bottleneck** – Very low promotion rate (only 72 employees).
3. **Long Commute Distance** – Nearly 70% of staff commute "very far".
4. **Low Employee Satisfaction** – Particularly in work relationships and job satisfaction.
5. **High Workload** – Majority of employees juggling 3+ job responsibilities.
6. **Limited High Performers** – Only 226 employees rated with 4 Stars.

---

## ✅ Solutions & Recommendations

### Immediate Actions
- **Review Retrenchment List**: Conduct a fair and transparent review of the 117 employees. Consider redeployment, upskilling, or voluntary exit packages instead of forced retrenchment where possible.
- **Accelerate Promotions**: Fast-track the 72 employees due for promotion to improve morale and retention.
- **Address Commute Issues**: Introduce remote/hybrid work options or transportation support for employees living very far.

### Short-term Recommendations
- Launch **targeted engagement programs** focused on improving Work Relationship and Job Satisfaction.
- Implement **workload balancing** to reduce employees handling 3+ job roles.
- Recognize and reward the 226 high-performing (4-Star) employees to encourage better performance culture.

### Long-term Strategies
- Develop a **robust talent management and succession planning** framework.
- Use this dashboard for **continuous monitoring** of key HR metrics.
- Conduct regular **employee satisfaction surveys** and link results to actionable improvements.
- Explore **skills development programs** to prepare employees for higher roles and reduce retrenchment needs.
